My Research Interests

  • High Energy Astrophysics
    • Short Gamma Ray Bursts (GRB), which are borne from neutron star-neutron star mergers and neutron star-black hole mergers with recent focus on GRB170817.
    • Astrophysical jets and their luminosity.
    • Monte Carlo simulations modeling the angular dependence in observations of kilonovae from compact binary mergers.
  • Astrobiology
    • Using stochastic processes to computationally model the emergence of life from non-living rarified environments.
    • Chirality and autocatalysis in the formation of early stage life.
  • Cryosphere
    • Ice sheet dynamics and simulating the fate of Icelandic glaciers using regional climate models and PISM code to project the mass loss forward in time.
  • Chaos Dynamics
    • Exploring complex networks and applications to climate in addition to stochastic processes far from thermodynamic equilibrium.

Projects

Ice Dynamics

This summer I will work with the wonderful Dr. Guðfinna Aðalgeirsdóttir to simulate the changing mass balance of Icelandic glaciers using PISM code! By applying a hydrodynamic correction to existing climate models, a better approximation can be obtained of the melting rate. I´m excited to learn more about the cryosphere! (2022)

Luminosity of Gamma Ray Bursts

The number of short Gamma-ray Bursts as a function of their jet luminosity is determined at the jet-launching site, not as the jets propagate. This internal effect has caused a significant reduction in their number as the luminosity gets higher. A collaboration with my amazing advisor, Dr. Barniol Duran, at California State University Sacramento for my Bachelor's thesis. (2021)
